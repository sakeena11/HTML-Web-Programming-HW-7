The Past & Present of COVID-19

Where It All Started

On December 31, 2019, China notified the World Health Organization (WHO) of an unknown pneumonia in the Hubei province in Wuhan. The symptoms included persistent fever,
cough, and dyspnea, and was then named Coronavirus Disease 2019, or COVID-19. The disease spread rapidly from Hubei province to other provinces in China within 2 weeks.
By November 14, 2020, there were a total of 92,409 COVID-19 cases and 4749 deaths in China. Less than 17% of the cases and less than 4% of the deaths occurred outside
of the Hubei province.

Starting January 13 2020, Thailand and more than 200 countries, including Japan, Korea, the United States, and the United Kingdom, reported imported COVID-19 cases. Due
to the speed and scale of transmission, the WHO described COVID-19 as a pandemic in March 2020 and officially declared it as a global epidemic.

COVID-19 During 2020

After the WHO declared COVID-19 as a global pandemic, the U.S. declares is as a national emergency and a Stay-At-Home order is issued. A mask mandate and guidelines are
also issues to decrease the spread of COVID-19. Hospitals start becoming filled with an overwhelming amount of COVID-19 patients, so the CARES Act is passed, which provides
$2 trillion in aid to hospitals, small businesses, as well as state and local governments.

Cases begin to rise, and trials continue in attempting to find and develop drugs to help treat COVID-19, as well vaccines to prevent against the virus. In May 2020, the U.S.
unemployment rate reaches 14.7%, which is the worst rate since the Great Depression. During the same month, the Coronavirus death toll surpasses 100,000 in the U.S. In
September 2021, the death rate in the U.S. surges to 200,000, and increased outbreaks continuing occurring due to large holiday gatherings.

COVID-19 Vaccines and Minor Variants

In December 2020, The first COVID-19 vaccine, Pfizer-BioNTech, is authorized, first for medical staff and essential workers, then to individuals who are 16 and older. Mid
December, death rates surpase 300,000, and the Moderna vaccine is authorized for individuals 18 and older. In February 2021, the one shot Johnson & Johnson vaccine is
authorized, but paused again in June 2021 due to concerns about blood clotting. At the end of June, the CDC continued recommending the Johnson & Johnson vaccine for
individuals 18 and older.

At the end of 2020, the first case of the COVID-19 UK variant in the U.S. is reported Colorado. In January 2021, a variant first discovered in Brazil is found in Minnesota.
